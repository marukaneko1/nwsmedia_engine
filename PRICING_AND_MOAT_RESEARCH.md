## Recommended Pricing

### Industry Landscape (2025)

| Tier | Who | Typical Range | Your Target Market |
|------|-----|---------------|--------------------|
| **DIY / Template** | Wix, Squarespace, Fiverr | $0–$2,000 | Low budget, low urgency |
| **Freelancer** | Solo designer/dev | $3,000–$8,000 (5–10 pages) | Price-sensitive, simple needs |
| **Boutique agency** | 1–5 person shop | $5,000–$15,000 | Your main competitor set |
| **Full-service agency** | 10+ people | $15,000–$50,000+ | Larger or enterprise |

### By Vertical (Your Focus)

| Vertical | Typical Investment | Notes |
|----------|--------------------|-------|
| **Contractors (HVAC, plumbing)** | $3,000–$12,000 | $6k–$8k is common for strong local sites |
| **Dentists** | $6,000–$25,000 | ADA cites ~$10k–$12k for solid dental sites |
| **Med spas / healthcare** | $2,500–$10,000 | $2.5k–$5k is a typical "sweet spot" |

### Suggested Structure

**Floor (minimum project):** $3,500–$4,000  
- Simple 5-page brochure site  
- SEO fundamentals, mobile-friendly  
- Keeps you out of race-to-the-bottom vs DIY/Fiverr  

**Target (main offer):** $5,500–$8,000  
- 8–12 pages  
- Local SEO built in  
- Audit data as proof and positioning  

**High end (complex needs):** $9,000–$12,000  
- Booking / intake forms  
- Multiple locations or services  
- More integrations and hand-holding  

**Ongoing:** $50–$150/month  
- Hosting, updates, backups  
- Small content edits  
- Optional local SEO light-touch support

## Your Advantages & Moat

### 1. Audit-First Selling

You pre-audit leads (PageSpeed, SEO, SSL, mobile) before you ever talk.

- You show specific issues and benchmarks, not vague promises.  
- 78% of complaints about web designers are about service, not tech; you look professional and prepared.  
- You can say: "Your speed is X, your competitors at Y. Half your mobile visitors leave before load."  

You compete on evidence, not just pitch.

---

### 2. Vertical Specialization

You focus on contractors, dentists, med spas in specific metros (Houston, Dallas, Atlanta, Phoenix, Austin).

- Agencies with a clear specialism outperform generalists (2x growth, 61%+ gross margin).  
- You understand reviews, local search, job value, and pain points.  
- You can cite benchmarks: "Dentists in Houston see…"  

You're the local specialist, not a generic "web designer."

---

### 3. Lead Engine (Pre-Qualified Pipeline)

Your system pre-filters and scores leads.

- You only talk to people who replied to cold outreach and booked.  
- You have triage, audit, and enrichment data before the call.  
- You know segment (ESTABLISHED vs NEW_SMALL) and can tailor positioning.  

You spend time on warmer, more qualified leads.

---

### 4. Built to Rank From Day One

Sites are built with local SEO in mind, not bolted on later.

- SEO can deliver 19.9x ROAS vs ~4.4x for ads for service businesses.  
- Cost per paying customer: ~$126 for SEO vs ~$553 for ads.  
- 88%+ of local mobile searches lead to call or visit within 24 hours.  

You sell "built to rank," not "we'll add SEO later."

---

### 5. Performance / Speed Focus

You work with PageSpeed and Core Web Vitals.

- ~1 sec slower load can hurt conversions ~7%.  
- 53% abandon after 3 seconds.  
- Fixing speed can improve conversions ~30–80%.  
- Template builders often sit at 3.5–5.5s vs a 2.5s benchmark.  

You differentiate on "built for speed," not only design.

---

### 6. "Owner-Operated" Boutique Positioning

You're a small, focused shop, not a big agency or random freelancer.

**vs freelancers:**  
- Direct contact to the person who actually does the work.  
- More predictable delivery.  
- You address the main fear: "designer disappears after launch."  

**vs big agencies:**  
- No bloated overhead.  
- Senior-level execution, not juniors behind account managers.  
- Faster decisions and clearer communication.  

---

### 7. Lead-Gen Alternatives (Thumbtack / HomeAdvisor)

Contractors pay per lead on Thumbtack/HomeAdvisor, often $50–$200+ per lead, with no quality guarantee.

- A $6,000 site at $100/lead ≈ 60 leads to break even.  
- Your site delivers leads 24/7 from Google and Maps with no per-lead fee.  
- Over 1–2 years, the math usually favors a proper site over paying platforms.  

---

### 8. ROI Framing by Vertical

- **Contractors:** "If your average job is $2k and the site brings 3 extra leads/month, that's $6k/month."  
- **Dentists:** "A $200 patient can be worth $5k+ over 5 years. SEO can do 3:1 to 5:1 returns, 10:1 over time."  
- **Med spas:** "A single procedure can be $500–$2k+. A few extra bookings/month pays for the site quickly."  

You sell outcomes, not just pages.

---

### 9. Niche Market Focus

Houston, Dallas, Atlanta, Phoenix, Austin = large, growing metros.

- You can name competitors and examples in their city.  
- You understand local search and local ranking.  
- You can say: "Top 3 [category] in [city] are ranking above you — here's why."  

---

### 10. Segment-Aware Messaging

You adjust by ESTABLISHED vs NEW_SMALL.

- **ESTABLISHED:** "Your reviews and reputation are strong; your site is holding you back."  
- **NEW_SMALL:** "You're early-stage; a site now builds credibility and captures searches as you grow."  

You're not pitching one generic offer.
